# Nation Wheel Wiki Study

Source studied: https://nation-wheel.fandom.com/wiki/Nation_Wheel_Wiki and its All Pages index.

## Core Canon

- Nation Wheel is a spin-based collaborative worldbuilding setting.
- One spin creates one canon entity and fills one fixed map location.
- Spin results, map placements, officially recorded events, and valid lore expansions are canon by default.
- Map placements are permanent. Borders, influence, governments, alliances, and status can evolve, but the filled location remains part of history.
- Season 1 is the foundational era where nations, borders, economies, governments, and early power dynamics are established.

## Product Implications

- The app should use the Season 1 map as a still reference image, with canon placement details maintained in lore/wiki text rather than interactive region data.
- Removing a nation should probably mean archiving or changing status in lore, not deleting canon history, once production rules are strict.
- Structured stats should stay narrow: name, slug, people, government, GDP, economy, and military.
- Political status, alliances, conflicts, bloc membership, colonies, protectorates, and ideological notes should be wiki/lore content, not fixed stat columns.
- LoreCP needs revision and moderation tools because canon changes must preserve continuity.
